All you need to know about Tally ERP 9

Before diving to it's other aspects let's first understand about "Tally ERP 9"

Tally Solutions Pvt. Ltd. created the integrated business accounting programme known as Tally ERP 9. Businesses use it extensively to manage their accounting, finance, inventory, taxation, payroll, and other related procedures.

Tally ERP 9 is an updated version of the original Tally software that is made to meet the needs of companies of all sizes and in a wide range of sectors.

Key features of Tally ERP 9 include:

Accounting and Financial management:

  • Tally ERP 9 includes a full range of accounting functions, including ledger administration, voucher input, bank reconciliation, balance sheets, profit and loss statements, and more.

Inventory management:

  • Companies can effectively manage their stock levels, keep track of sales and purchases, and produce stock reports to keep an eye on inventory movement.

Taxation:

  • Tally ERP 9 is prepared to manage a variety of taxation requirements, including GST (Goods and Services Tax) for firms in nations like India, VAT (Value Added Tax), TDS (Tax Deducted at Source), and others.

Payroll management:

  • The programme can be used to keep track of employee information, attendance, and wage calculations, as well as to produce pay stubs.

Financial Analysis and Reporting:

  • Tally ERP 9 offers a variety of financial reports to help with decision-making, including cash flow statements, fund flow statements, aging analysis, and ratio analysis.

Multi-currency Support:

  • Companies that conduct worldwide business may track and manage transactions in several currencies.

In order to learn how to use Tally ERP 9, a well-known accounting programme created by Tally Solutions Pvt. Ltd., individuals must take a Tally course, which is a structured training programme.

The purpose of the course is to provide students with the information and abilities needed to effectively handle accounting, finance, inventory, taxation, payroll, and other business-related procedures using Tally software.

Key features of a Tally course include:

Curriculum:

  • The course covers a variety of Tally ERP 9-related topics, starting with the fundamentals of accounting and working its way up to more complex capabilities.
  • Ledger administration, voucher entry, inventory management, taxation (such as GST), financial reporting, payroll, and other topics are frequently covered in the curriculum.

Hands-on instruction:

  • Tally courses frequently include hands-on instruction, giving students the chance to practice using the programme and complete actual accounting tasks
  • Practical experience improves knowledge of and skill using Tally.

Certificate:

  • After completing one of the reputable Tally courses, the student receives a certificate verifying their abilities. This qualification may be useful when looking for work or proving your competence to potential customers.

Practical Projects:

  • Some courses may include practical projects that mimic real business scenarios to help students use their Tally abilities in real-world settings.

GST Compliance:

  • Many Tally courses emphasize teaching learners how to handle GST-related transactions and invoicing, given the significance of GST (Goods and Services Tax) in modern taxation systems.

How long does it take to complete a Tally course?

The duration of a Tally course can vary based on various factors, including the level of the course, the depth of the curriculum, the mode of training (online or classroom), and the institute or training provider.

Generally, Tally courses are designed to be flexible, allowing learners to complete them at their own pace. Here are some common variations of Tally courses and their approximate durations:

Basic Tally Course:

  • A basic Tally course, covering fundamental concepts and features, can typically be completed in 15 to 30 hours of study, which could be spread over a few days to a few weeks.

Comprehensive Tally Course:

  • A more comprehensive Tally course that covers all major features, including advanced topics like GST and payroll, may take around 30 to 60 hours of study. This duration may be spread over a few weeks to a few months.

Tally Expert Course:

  • An advanced Tally course aimed at becoming an expert user and handling complex scenarios may span anywhere from 60 to 100 hours of study, and the duration could be several weeks or a few months.

Fast-track or Intensive Courses:

  • Some institutes offer fast-track or intensive Tally courses, which condense the content into a shorter duration, such as a week-long or weekend course.
  • These courses may require more dedication and focused learning.

Online Self-paced Courses:

  • Online Tally courses often allow learners to study at their own pace, and the duration can vary depending on the time the learner invests daily or weekly. It could range from a few weeks to a few months.
